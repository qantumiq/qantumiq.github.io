Why Most AI Initiatives Stall

Only 10-15% of AI projects reach production. The gap stems from organizational issues: unclear objectives, insufficient data infrastructure, talent gaps, and lack of systematic approaches to operationalizing AI.

Phase 1: Strategy & Opportunity Assessment

Identify, evaluate, and prioritize AI use cases by business impact, technical feasibility, data readiness, and organizational capacity. Each should have a business sponsor, success metrics, and ROI timeline.

Phase 2: Data Foundation

Build modern data platforms ‚Äî lakes, feature stores, streaming pipelines ‚Äî with governance, quality standards, lineage tracking, and privacy compliance.

Phase 3: Proof of Value

Design PoVs with production deployment in mind, using representative data and realistic benchmarks. Validate business value, not just technical performance.

Phase 4: Production Deployment

Requires MLOps (automated pipelines, monitoring, versioning), system integration, and thoughtful human-AI interaction design for adoption.

Phase 5: Scaling

Establish AI Centers of Excellence sharing best practices and reusable components. At scale, new AI projects take 60-70% less time than initial deployments.
